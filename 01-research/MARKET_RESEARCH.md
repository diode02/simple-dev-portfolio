# Market Research

## Website Type

This project is a simple one-page personal portfolio website for a software developer. It should work as a concise professional profile, selected work showcase, and contact entry point.

## Target Users

- Hiring managers and recruiters quickly evaluating fit for a software role.
- Engineering managers looking for evidence of practical project experience.
- Potential freelance or contract clients who need to understand skills and availability.
- Peers or collaborators who want a quick view of background, tools, and projects.

## User Intent

Visitors usually want to answer these questions within a short visit:

- Who is this developer?
- What kind of work do they do?
- What technologies are they comfortable with?
- Have they built relevant projects?
- How can I contact them or view their work elsewhere?

## Market And Competitor Observations

Common strong developer portfolios are:

- Fast to scan, with a clear name, role, and value statement visible immediately.
- Focused on proof of work through a few well-described projects instead of long biography.
- Simple and professional rather than visually noisy.
- Responsive across mobile and desktop because recruiters often open links from email or mobile apps.
- Easy to contact, with visible email and professional links.
- Built with clean spacing, readable typography, and restrained animation.

Common weak portfolio patterns to avoid:

- Generic hero text that does not clearly say what the developer does.
- Too many sections, effects, or dense paragraphs for a simple portfolio.
- Project cards that only show titles without context, technologies, or links.
- Contact forms without clear fallback contact information.
- Low contrast text, tiny body copy, or layouts that break on mobile.

## Positioning Recommendation

The site should present the developer as clear, reliable, and practical. The design should feel modern and polished but not overbuilt. The strongest first impression should come from clear content hierarchy, thoughtful spacing, and credible project summaries.

## Conversion Goal

The primary conversion goal is for a visitor to initiate contact or continue evaluating the developer through a professional link.

Primary actions:

- Email the developer.
- Open GitHub.
- Open LinkedIn.

Secondary actions:

- View project demos or source links.
- Navigate directly to sections using the page navigation.

## SEO Basics

The site should be discoverable for the developer's name and role. It should include:

- Descriptive page title such as "Alex Carter - Software Developer Portfolio".
- Meta description summarizing the developer's role, skills, and selected work.
- One clear H1 with the developer's name and role context.
- Semantic section headings for About, Skills, Projects, and Contact.
- Meaningful link text instead of "click here".
- Open Graph metadata for sharing, if the build stack supports it easily.

## Accessibility Basics

The portfolio should follow baseline accessibility expectations:

- Semantic HTML landmarks and headings.
- Keyboard-accessible navigation and links.
- Visible focus states.
- Sufficient color contrast for text and interactive elements.
- Responsive text and layouts without horizontal scrolling.
- Descriptive labels for icon-only links.
- Alt text for any meaningful imagery.
