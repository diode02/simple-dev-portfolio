# Acceptance Criteria

## Page Structure

- The website is a single-page software developer portfolio.
- The page includes header/navigation, hero, about, skills, projects, and contact sections.
- Navigation links move to the correct sections on the same page.
- The hero includes name, title, short intro, and at least one clear call to action.
- The projects section includes exactly 3 project entries.

## Content

- Placeholder developer content is realistic, professional, and easy to replace.
- No unsupported claims are made about employers, education, awards, or years of experience.
- Each project includes a title, short description, and technology list.
- Contact information includes at least an email placeholder plus GitHub and LinkedIn links.

## Design

- The design is clean, modern, and appropriate for a software developer portfolio.
- The page has clear visual hierarchy and readable body text.
- Project cards or project entries are visually distinct and scannable.
- Buttons and links have hover and focus states.
- The page avoids unnecessary product complexity beyond the one-page portfolio scope.

## Responsiveness

- The site works at common mobile, tablet, and desktop widths.
- Text does not overlap or overflow its containers.
- Navigation remains usable on small screens.
- The page does not create horizontal scrolling on mobile.

## Accessibility

- The page uses semantic headings in logical order.
- There is only one H1.
- Interactive elements are keyboard accessible.
- Visible focus styling is present for links and buttons.
- Text contrast is sufficient for comfortable reading.
- Icon-only links, if used, have accessible labels.

## SEO

- The document has a descriptive title.
- The document has a relevant meta description.
- Section IDs and headings support basic search indexing.
- Link text is meaningful and understandable out of context.

## Technical

- The project can be run or opened locally according to the builder's chosen setup.
- The page does not require a backend service.
- The implementation should keep dependencies minimal for the simple scope.
- No build-breaking console errors should appear during normal viewing.
